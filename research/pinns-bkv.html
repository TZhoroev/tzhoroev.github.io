Interpretable Discovery of Hidden Dynamics in BK Virus Infection

NC State University (PhD Dissertation)

This dissertation research combined physics-informed neural networks, causal reasoning, and symbolic regression to recover interpretable differential-equation structure from hidden BK virus infection dynamics in renal transplantation.

Research Overview

BK virus infection after kidney transplantation can trigger complex changes in serum creatinine, a clinically important marker of renal function. This work aimed to recover the hidden dynamical mechanisms governing those changes by combining flexible neural models with explicit mathematical structure.

The research integrated Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), causal inference, and symbolic regression. PINNs were used to fit noisy clinical trajectories while respecting ordinary differential equation constraints, and symbolic regression was then used to distill black-box behavior into interpretable ODE terms.

This project formed part of the dissertation “Developing Gaussian Process and Theory-Informed Neural Network Models for Clinical Decision Making” (2025) at NC State University, advised by Dr. Kevin Flores. The broader goal was to move from accurate prediction alone toward mechanistic understanding that clinicians can inspect and trust.

Core Contributions

  • Integrated PINNs, causal inference, and symbolic regression in a unified workflow for hidden-dynamics discovery.
  • Modeled serum-creatinine ODE dynamics arising after BK virus infection in renal transplantation.
  • Extracted interpretable governing terms from black-box neural network surrogates rather than stopping at predictive accuracy.
  • Positioned neural differential-equation modeling as a tool for clinically meaningful, hypothesis-generating discovery.

Mathematical Formulation

PINNs embed mechanistic structure into the training objective by penalizing violations of governing equations. This makes the learned representation data-consistent while still honoring prior scientific knowledge about the dynamics.

\[\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{PINN}} = \mathrm{MSE}_{\mathrm{data}} + \lambda\,\mathrm{MSE}_{\mathrm{physics}}\]Composite loss balancing data fidelity against the residual of the governing ODE constraints.
\[\mathrm{MSE}_{\mathrm{physics}} = \frac{1}{N_r}\sum_{i=1}^{N_r}\left\lVert \dot{c}(t_i) - f_{\theta}(c(t_i), t_i, u(t_i))\right\rVert^2\]Physics residual enforcing agreement between the neural network representation and the serum-creatinine dynamical model.

Why it matters

By pairing theory-informed learning with symbolic regression, the project supports a shift from opaque predictive models toward interpretable, mechanistic insights that can help generate clinically actionable hypotheses.

Methodology

Physics-Informed Neural Networks

The neural model was trained on observed trajectories while penalizing violations of the candidate ODE system, ensuring that learned dynamics remained scientifically grounded.

Causal and Mechanistic Framing

Causal inference concepts guided which variables and interactions should be interpreted as potential drivers of renal-function change rather than mere correlates.

Symbolic Regression for Equation Recovery

After fitting the black-box surrogate, symbolic regression searched for concise analytical expressions that reproduced the learned dynamics in an interpretable ODE form.

Research Detail

Alzheimer's Disease Progression Modeling

Johnson & Johnson (Summer Internship 2023)

During a Summer 2023 internship at Johnson & Johnson, variational Gaussian Process models with ordinal likelihood were developed to predict ADAS cognitive scores and quantify uncertainty in Alzheimer's disease progression.

Research Overview

Alzheimer’s disease progression is heterogeneous, making it difficult to forecast cognitive decline with a single deterministic trajectory. This project addressed that challenge with variational Gaussian Process models that produced both predictions and uncertainty estimates for longitudinal cognitive outcomes.

The modeling target was ADAS cognitive score progression, using clinical trial data from 1,683 patients. An ordinal likelihood formulation was used to better respect the structure of observed outcomes, while a variational approximation enabled scalable learning for several competing model choices.

Five distinct model configurations were compared using 5-fold cross-validation, allowing the internship study to evaluate trade-offs in fit, calibration, and predictive robustness. The project emphasized uncertainty quantification as a core requirement for translating disease-progression models into decision-support settings.

Core Contributions

  • Developed variational Gaussian Process models tailored to ordinal clinical outcomes in Alzheimer’s disease.
  • Predicted ADAS cognitive scores while quantifying uncertainty in patient-specific disease trajectories.
  • Benchmarked five modeling configurations with 5-fold cross-validation on 1,683 patients.
  • Positioned probabilistic progression models as more informative than single-value forecasts for clinical research workflows.

Mathematical Formulation

Variational inference provided a tractable route to fitting Gaussian Process models with non-Gaussian likelihoods. The objective balances data fit under the approximate posterior with a regularization term that keeps the variational family close to the GP prior.

\[\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}} = \mathbb{E}_{q(f)}\big[\log p(y\mid f)\big] - \mathrm{KL}\big(q(f)\,\|\,p(f)\big)\]Evidence lower bound used to optimize the variational posterior for ordinal-outcome Gaussian Process models.
\[p(y_i = k\mid f_i) = g(\tau_k - f_i) - g(\tau_{k-1} - f_i)\]Ordinal likelihood structure that maps latent GP function values to ordered cognitive-score categories through thresholds.

Why it matters

Uncertainty-aware progression modeling can help distinguish confident forecasts from ambiguous ones, which is especially important when clinical trial populations are heterogeneous and downstream decisions depend on risk-sensitive interpretation.

Methodology

Variational Gaussian Processes

A variational approximation made it feasible to fit flexible nonparametric models while preserving the Bayesian uncertainty quantification that is critical in disease progression analysis.

Ordinal Outcome Modeling

Ordinal likelihoods respected the structure of cognitive scoring and reduced the mismatch that can arise when discrete or ordered outcomes are treated as purely continuous.

Cross-Validated Model Comparison

Five candidate configurations were assessed with 5-fold cross-validation to compare predictive performance, uncertainty behavior, and robustness across patient subsets.

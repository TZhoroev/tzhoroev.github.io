Clinical modeling assumptions

Let \(x_i\) denote covariates and visit-time information for patient \(i\), and let \(y_i\) denote the ordered ADAS outcome. The internship study treated progression as a latent smooth function observed with uncertainty, while explicitly accounting for the fact that ordered outcomes should not be handled as if they were unconstrained real values.

That perspective makes two modeling layers essential: a Gaussian Process prior for flexible longitudinal structure and an ordinal observation model for clinically meaningful category boundaries.

Internship focus

The goal was comparative evaluation of GP model configurations for longitudinal ADAS prediction. This work focused on patient scale, validation rigor, and the role of ordinal likelihood in uncertainty-aware disease progression modeling.

Five-Configuration Comparison

The internship summary reports five compared configurations. The table below highlights their comparative role without relying on internal code names.

Configuration Modeling focus Purpose in the study
Configuration 1 Continuous-response GP baseline Establish a smooth probabilistic benchmark for ADAS trajectories.
Configuration 2 Sparse GP approximation Test whether computational simplification changes the quality of uncertainty estimates.
Configuration 3 Variational GP Support scalable approximate inference across repeated cross-validation splits.
Configuration 4 Variational GP with ordinal likelihood Respect the ordered structure of observed cognitive outcomes.
Configuration 5 Final uncertainty-aware comparison candidate Contrast calibration, interpretability, and predictive stability against the other four settings.

Gaussian Process posterior

\[ \mu(x_*) = K(x_*, X)\left[K(X,X) + \sigma^2 I\right]^{-1} y \]
\[ \operatorname{Var}(f_*) = K(x_*, x_*) - K(x_*, X)\left[K(X,X) + \sigma^2 I\right]^{-1}K(X, x_*) \]

The posterior mean and variance summarize expected cognitive progression together with confidence in that forecast.

Ordinal likelihood

\[ p(y_i = k \mid f_i) = g\big(\tau_k - f_i\big) - g\big(\tau_{k-1} - f_i\big), \qquad \tau_0 < \tau_1 < \cdots < \tau_K \]

The thresholds \(\tau_k\) encode ordered ADAS categories, while the link function \(g\) converts the latent GP output into category probabilities.

Discrete Fractional Pharmacokinetics Modeling

Western Kentucky University

This project studies pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic dynamics through the lens of discrete fractional calculus. The emphasis is mathematical first: define the nabla operators carefully, state the assumptions clearly, and then use that framework to reason about memory, controllability, and observability.

Why fractional operators matter

Classical discrete differences depend only on nearby states, but pharmacological systems often retain memory of earlier exposure. Nabla fractional operators encode that history directly, making them natural tools for discrete PK/PD models with hereditary effects.

Prominent Nabla Definitions

Backward difference

\[ \nabla x(t) = x(t) - x(t-1) \]

This operator is the discrete-time analogue of a first derivative and is the building block for the fractional constructions that follow.

Fractional nabla sum

\[ \nabla_a^{-\alpha} x(t) = \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha)} \sum_{s=a+1}^{t} \big(t-\rho(s)\big)^{\overline{\alpha-1}} x(s), \qquad \rho(s)=s-1 \]

The fractional sum aggregates prior states with weights determined by the rising factorial kernel, making memory explicit.

Fractional nabla difference

\[ \nabla_a^{\alpha} x(t) = \nabla^{n}\,\nabla_a^{-(n-\alpha)}x(t), \qquad n-1 < \alpha < n \]

This definition extends integer-order differencing to non-integer order \(\alpha\), which is the key step in fractional discrete dynamics.

Modeling assumptions

To keep notation consistent, the page assumes:

  • time is sampled on a discrete grid \(t \in \mathbb{N}_a\);
  • the fractional order satisfies \(0 < \alpha < 1\) unless otherwise noted;
  • state variables represent PK/PD quantities whose current behavior can depend on the full history encoded by the nabla kernel.
\[ \nabla_a^{\alpha} x(t+1) = A x(t) + B u(t), \qquad y(t) = C x(t) \]

This state-space form is the entry point for controllability and observability analysis in linear nabla fractional systems.

Application context

The mathematical framework supports discrete and discrete fractional PK/PD models for tumor growth and anti-cancer treatment response. The scientific contribution lies in connecting rigorous operator definitions to biologically motivated models with memory.

The work was supported by the Kentucky Science and Engineering Foundation and culminated in both a journal paper and a master's thesis.

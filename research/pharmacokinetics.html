Research Overview

This work explored discrete and discrete fractional pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic models for tumor growth and anti-cancer treatment effects. The central mathematical theme was the use of nabla fractional calculus to capture memory effects and nonlocal dynamics that are not represented in classical integer-order formulations.

Beyond model formulation, the research investigated foundational systems questions for linear nabla fractional discrete systems, including controllability and observability. These properties are essential for understanding whether system states can be reconstructed from measurements and influenced through external inputs.

The project was supported by the Kentucky Science and Engineering Foundation (KSEF). Its outcomes contributed to both a journal publication and the master’s thesis “Controllability and Observability of Linear Nabla Discrete Fractional Systems” (2019).

Core Contributions

  • Studied discrete and discrete fractional PK/PD models for tumor growth and anti-cancer effects.
  • Used nabla fractional calculus operators to model systems with memory and hereditary structure.
  • Established controllability and observability conditions for linear nabla fractional discrete systems.
  • Connected mathematical systems theory with biologically motivated pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic modeling.

Mathematical Formulation

Fractional difference operators generalize classical finite differences by allowing non-integer orders, which makes them well suited for systems with long-memory effects. In the nabla setting, these operators naturally support backward-difference formulations on discrete time domains.

\[\nabla^{\alpha} x_k = \sum_{j=0}^{k} (-1)^j \binom{\alpha}{j} x_{k-j}, \qquad 0 < \alpha < 1\]A representative nabla fractional difference operator capturing nonlocal memory effects in discrete-time dynamics.
\[\nabla^{\alpha} x_{k+1} = A x_k + B u_k, \qquad y_k = C x_k\]State-space form used to analyze controllability and observability of linear nabla fractional discrete systems.

Systems-Theoretic Analysis

Biophysical Interpretation

Uncertainty Quantification for Physical & Biological Models

NC State University · National Science Foundation

🔬 NSF-Funded Research at North Carolina State University

Employed uncertainty propagation techniques to detect discrepancies between mathematical models of physical and biological systems and observational data, and to estimate the associated uncertainty. Developed a Bayesian method for simultaneous interval prediction, addressing uncertainty in the extrapolation domain to facilitate informed decision-making.

Six Project Areas

Sensitivity Analysis

Local and global techniques for ranking influential inputs before expensive calibration or simulation campaigns.

Parameter Estimation

Deterministic fitting workflows for matching model outputs to observed data under explicit objective functions.

MCMC Sampling

Posterior exploration tools for Bayesian calibration, including proposal-based Markov chain methods.

Frequentist Intervals

Confidence and prediction interval workflows for quantifying uncertainty without a full posterior model.

Surrogate Modeling

Reduced-cost approximations for expensive simulators, enabling faster screening and inference.

Model Discrepancy Detection

Diagnostics for identifying when mismatch is due to structural inadequacy rather than parameter uncertainty alone.

Bayesian core

Assume a forward model \(y = \mathcal{M}(\theta) + \varepsilon\) with prior information on \(\theta\). Posterior calibration begins with Bayes' theorem:

\[ p(\theta \mid y) = \frac{p(y \mid \theta)\,p(\theta)}{p(y)} \]

This formulation turns parameter estimation into posterior inference once model error and prior knowledge are specified.

MCMC acceptance ratio

For a proposal \(\theta' \sim q(\theta'\mid\theta)\), a Metropolis-Hastings step accepts the move with probability

\[ \alpha(\theta, \theta') = \min\!\left(1, \frac{p(y\mid\theta')p(\theta')q(\theta\mid\theta')}{p(y\mid\theta)p(\theta)q(\theta'\mid\theta)}\right). \]

This ratio appears throughout the portfolio whenever posterior samples are needed instead of point estimates.

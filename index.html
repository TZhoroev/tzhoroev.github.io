About Me

I am a Data Scientist at Microsoft with a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from North Carolina State University. My research focuses on developing Gaussian Process and Physics-Informed Neural Network models for clinical decision making.

I am passionate about applying rigorous mathematical methods to solve real-world problems, particularly at the intersection of machine learning, statistics, and scientific computing. My work spans uncertainty quantification, Bayesian inference, and computational modeling of physical and biological systems.

Research

Labor Progression Prediction

Developed novel machine learning models using Gaussian Processes to predict labor progression, improving clinical decision-making in obstetrics. Published in American Journal of Obstetrics and Gynecology.

Physics-Informed Neural Networks

Created hybrid ML frameworks combining PINNs with causal inference and symbolic regression for modeling biological systems, enabling interpretable and uncertainty-aware predictions.

Uncertainty Quantification

Applied Bayesian approaches for uncertainty propagation in mathematical models, detecting discrepancies between model predictions and observational data in physical and biological systems.

Pharmacokinetics Modeling

Studied discrete and discrete fractional pharmacokinetics-pharmacodynamics models for tumor growth and anti-cancer effects using fractional calculus.

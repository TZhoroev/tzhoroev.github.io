About Me

I am a Data Scientist at Microsoft with a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from North Carolina State University. My research focuses on developing Gaussian Process and Physics-Informed Neural Network models for clinical decision making.

I am passionate about applying rigorous mathematical methods to solve real-world problems, particularly at the intersection of machine learning, statistics, and scientific computing. My work spans uncertainty quantification, Bayesian inference, and computational modeling of physical and biological systems.

Research

PeriGen / NIH / NICHD

Labor Progression Prediction

Developed Sparse Gaussian Process models with meta-learning to predict cervical dilation and fetal station in real time, improving clinical decision-making in obstetrics. Validated on 49,694 births, demonstrating superiority over WHO and ACOG guidelines.

Read more
NC State University (PhD)

Interpretable Discovery of Hidden Dynamics in BKV Infection

Integrated Physics-Informed Neural Networks, causal inference, and symbolic regression to uncover hidden creatinine dynamics in BK virus infection after kidney transplantation. Extracted interpretable ODE terms from black-box models.

Read more
Johnson & Johnson

Alzheimer's Disease Progression Modeling

Developed Variational Gaussian Process models with ordinal likelihood to predict ADAS cognitive scores and quantify uncertainty in Alzheimer's disease progression, using clinical trial data from 1,683 patients.

Read more
NC State University

Uncertainty Quantification

Comprehensive implementations of UQ methods: sensitivity analysis, parameter estimation, MCMC sampling, uncertainty propagation, surrogate models, and model discrepancy detection with Python and MATLAB.

Read more
Western Kentucky University

Discrete Fractional Pharmacokinetics Modeling

Studied discrete and discrete fractional PK/PD models for tumor growth and anti-cancer effects using nabla fractional calculus. Established controllability and observability conditions for fractional systems. Funded by KSEF.

About Me

I'm a Data Scientist at Microsoft and hold a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from North Carolina State University. What drives my work is a simple question: when a model makes a prediction, how much should we trust it?

During my Ph.D., I built Gaussian process and physics-informed neural network models to help clinicians — predicting how labor will progress for individual patients, and uncovering hidden infection dynamics in kidney transplant recipients from sparse clinical records. Along the way, I've also worked on Alzheimer's disease progression forecasting at Johnson & Johnson, hotel demand prediction at SAS, and Bayesian uncertainty quantification funded by the NSF. Each project reinforced the same lesson: the uncertainty around a prediction is often more valuable than the prediction itself.
